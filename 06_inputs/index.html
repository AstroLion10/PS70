Below is a photo of my circuit configuration: </p> <image style = "width: 50%"src = "../06_inputs/wk6CapSensorCircuit.jpg" alt = "Capacitive Sensor Circuit"> </image> <h4>Assignment 2: Flex/Pressure Sensor</h4> <p class="margin"> I created a velostat flex sensor to use for the soft robotic glove. This sensor can help with finger motion tracking in addition to glove actuator automation for the final project. </p> <p class="margin"> To make the sensor, I followed instructions on <a href="https://lucstechblog.blogspot.com/2018/04/flex-and-pressure-sensors.html">Lucs Tech Blog</a>. First, I took a piece of masking tape. Then, I cut a short length of copper tape and gently folded the sticky side onto itself to make a flexible, thin copper plate. I centered this copper piece on the tape. After this, I cut two identical small velostat rectangles. Then, I placed one rectangle on top of the copper plate (the velostat should be wider and cover the whole copper plate). I added the other piece of velostat on top of the first and placed the next copper plate (making sure it does not touch the first copper plate). Lastly, I sealed the other side with masking tape. This made the finished sensor. I would recommend using a more insulating tape material for future velostat sensors, as touching the paper masking tape with a conductive hand might potentially affect the sensor reading. Here is the finished sensor: </p> <image style = "width: 50%"src = "../06_inputs/wk6FlexSensor1.jpg" alt = "Flex Sensor"> </image> <p class="margin"> To test it, I hooked up both of the copper ends to a multimeter and measured the sensor's resistance when it was bent at different angles. </p> <image style = "width: 50%"src = "../06_inputs/wk6FlexMultimeter.jpg" alt = "Multimeter"> </image> <p class="margin"> Sources used: <br></br> https://nathanmelenbrink.github.io/ps70/06_input/index.html <br></br> https://lucstechblog.blogspot.com/2018/04/flex-and-pressure-sensors.html <br></br> https://cdn-shop.adafruit.com/datasheets/HandcraftingSensors.pdf <br></br> </p> </div>
